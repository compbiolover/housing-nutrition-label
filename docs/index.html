Housing Nutrition Label

An open-source platform that scores residential properties on disaster resilience, energy efficiency, infrastructure burden, health impact, socioeconomic factors, and walkability — like a nutrition label for your home.

View on GitHub

The Problem

There is a massive information asymmetry in housing markets. Buyers cannot evaluate construction quality, lifecycle costs, disaster resilience, or the true cost of ownership. Builders have no incentive to build well because the market cannot distinguish quality. Cities cannot demonstrate the true infrastructure cost differential between dense infill and sprawl.

Nobody has built a unified, open-source, multi-dimensional lifecycle scoring platform with API distribution to listing platforms. The field is wide open.

The Vision

A standardized, color-coded scorecard for every property — showing how it performs across multiple dimensions that matter to buyers, communities, and cities. Each dimension scored 0–100 with dual grades: a national absolute grade and a local percentile-based grade for within-market comparison.

Scored Dimensions

🏗

Disaster Resilience

Expected Annual Loss (EAL) across flood, tornado, earthquake, and fire hazards. Adjusted for construction type, building code era, foundation, and condition using a Building Resilience Modifier with literature-backed ICF and FORTIFIED Home modifiers.

⚡

Energy Efficiency

Energy Use Intensity (kBTU/sqft/yr) based on DOE/EIA ResStock benchmarks, adjusted for building vintage, construction type, and HVAC system. Estimated monthly cost at Memphis/TVA utility rates.

🏘

Infrastructure Burden

Annual municipal cost to serve each parcel (roads, water/sewer, fire, police, sanitation) based on density. Fiscal ratio shows property tax revenue vs. infrastructure cost — revealing which properties pay their way.

❤

Health Impact

CDC PLACES census-tract health metrics: obesity, diabetes, high blood pressure, physical inactivity, mental distress, asthma, coronary heart disease, stroke, and COPD prevalence. Indexed 0–100.

📊

Socioeconomic

Census ACS indicators including median household income, poverty rate, educational attainment, and unemployment by census tract. Reflects neighborhood economic opportunity.

🚶

Walkability

Walk Score API walk, transit, and bike scores. The 0–100 Walk Score is used directly; where transit and bike scores exist, a composite is taken (60% walk + 25% transit + 15% bike), weighted toward walkability since it matters most for daily errands.

🌡

Climate Projections

NOAA 1991–2020 normals: heating/cooling degree days, extreme heat days, freeze days, annual precipitation. Placeholder for forward-looking climate model projections. (Uniform for single-county pilot.)

Dual Grading System

Every dimension produces two grades, because context matters:

National Grade

Absolute thresholds (A≥80, B≥60, C≥40, D≥20, F<20). Tells you how a Memphis property compares to the rest of the country. Useful for cross-city comparisons.

A B C D F

Local Grade

Percentile-based within Shelby County (A=top 10%, B=next 25%, C=next 30%, D=next 25%, F=bottom 10%). Tells you how a property compares to its neighbors. Useful for home-shopping.

A B C D F

Current Status

Phase 1 complete: 1,000 Shelby County (Memphis, TN) parcels scored across 6 dimensions with real data from 10 public data sources. Includes a CLI house simulator with 8 presets supporting multi-unit buildings (duplexes, quadplexes) and 20+ above-code construction features.

The scoring engine, data pipeline, and simulator are fully open-source under the MIT license.
